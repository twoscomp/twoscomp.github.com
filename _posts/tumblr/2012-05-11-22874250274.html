--- layout: post title: Eerily Familiar Laughter ---

So I think there is a distinct sonic difference between when a group of American adults laugh and when a group of Asian adults laugh. Growing up, my parents always hosted pot lucks and bible studies at our house and I got used to listening to the way they burst into extremely tightly executed roars of laughter. I would characterize Asian laughs as very short, quick, and controlled roll up and roll down of laughing. I think American laughter is more free form and unique, where you can pick out each person’s characteristics and not everyone stops laughing at exactly the same time. After having been away from home after leaving for college so many years ago it wasn’t until the end of the day today that I heard a similar Asian sound signature coming from the conference room here at Samsung. lol weird.
